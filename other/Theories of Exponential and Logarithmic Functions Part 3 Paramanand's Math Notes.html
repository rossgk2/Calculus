In this concluding post on the theories of exponential and logarithmic functions we will present the most intuitive and obvious approach to define the expression abab directly without going to the number ee and the function logxlogx. This approach stems from the fact that an irrational number can be approximated by rational numbers and we can find as good approximations as we want. The idea is that if bb is irrational and a>0a>0 then we have many rational approximations b,b,b,b, to bb and the numbers ab,ab,ab,ab, would be the approximations to the number abab being defined.
